Commute Options

Transportation Services manages many programs to promote alternatives to driving. Whether you are a commuter student driving to school or a resident traveling off campus, we can help you get there.  For help navigating your options, please contact Transportation Services at (951) 827-8277 or email alttransportation@ucr.edu.

  • Biking & Walking

    Riding a bike or walking is fun, healthy, and environmentally friendly commute mode. There are over 3,000 bike racks spaces located throughout campus. There are also four locked bike cages available at no cost for those that have registered their bikes on campus.

    Bike amenities: UCR has several self-fix-it stations for do-it-yourself light repairs. Pedals bike shop is also conveniently located on campus every Wednesday 10:00 AM - 4:00 PM in Lot 19, offering repairs, parts and accessories. Transportation Services provides free bike registration and issues bike licenses daily between the hours of 8:00 AM - 4:00 PM.

    If you are a UCR campus employee or graduate student, you may want to explore incentives for biking or walking to campus. Visit our Biking/Walking page.

  • Riverside Transit Agency - UPASS Program

    UCR subsidizes 100% of your Riverside Transit Agency (RTA) bus pass through RTA's U-PASS Program. Register  for a free mobile pass and show your mobile device on board for unlimited, FREE rides on any RTA fixed route.  All RTA buses come equipped with free Wi-Fi and USB charging ports. Current RTA schedules are available online or at Transportation Services.

    If you are a UCR campus employee or graduate student, you may want to explore incentives for taking the bus to campus. Visit our Learn About Taking Bus page.

  • Carsharing

    Carsharing is available on campus!

    Zipcar is a self-service car share program available to the entire campus community. Zipcar vehicles are available right on campus and can be reserved by the hour or day 24/7. Rental fees include insurance, gas and mileage up to 180 miles per day. Rentals are meant for roundtrips and can be reserved in advance when you need to run errands, attend meetings, visit friends or anything else you may need to do.

    Zipcars can be found in reserved areas in Parking Lot 1, Bannockburn, and the auto courts just outside of Big Springs Parking Structure 1.

    How do I join?

    You must first register for a membership at Zipcar by using your UCR email account. UCR students and staff benefit from a discounted $15 annual membership fee to participate. You must be at least 18 years old with a valid driver's license and credit card to join. International licenses are acceptable. Once approved by Zipcar, you will be mailed a membership card to access the cars or you can instantly reserve by using the Zipcar app.

    Rates vary depending on the vehicle, so check the Zipcar website to view the current inventory of vehicles and pricing.

  • Find a carpool partner

    Find a carpool partner!

    UC Riverside has partnered with Waze Carpool.  Choose to drive or ride, and match with people based on route and time of day.

    Waze Carpool can also help both students and employees find someone to share the ride with to campus. Download the Waze Carpool app (iOS/Android) and be sure to add your campus email address to your profile so that you are matched with others in the UCR community.

    Faculty, staff and graduate students can also register online for a free IE Commuter account and they will match you up with others interested in sharing a ride with people who live and work near you with similar hours. You may also qualify for County sponsored subsidies.  For more information Visit our Learn About Carpooling page.

  • Metrolink

    Metrolink’s train services provides connections in six Southern California counties with two local stations near UCR.  Metrolink offers a 25% discount to students with a valid student ID.  RTA has several bus routes that run from the Metrolink station directly to campus.

  • Vanpool

    Vanpooling is a cost effective option available to all members of the UCR community that commute to campus regularly. Routes operate Monday-Friday, traveling between common pickup locations and UCR's campus. They run once per day, usually arriving before 8:00 AM and leaving the campus by 5:00 PM. Prices vary depending on the distance of the route and number of riders. A monthly charge is assessed to all vanpool riders regardless of the number of days they ride. Start by going to Commute with Enterprise and clicking on "Join Commute".

    If you are a UCR campus employee or graduate student, you may want to explore incentives for vanpooling to campus. Visit our Learn About Vanpooling page.

Parking Options

If you choose to drive to campus, a valid UCR parking permit must be associated with your vehicle license plate at all times on the UC Riverside campus. Faculty/ Staff parking permits may be purchased at the Parking Services office.  Most permits are lot specific and sold based on lot availability. Waiting lists are maintained for high demand lots. You may add/remove yourself from waiting lists via our Parking Account Center by logging in with your NetID.

Parking permits may be purchased using check, Visa, Mastercard, or Discover. Qualified employees may also pay permit fees via monthly payroll deduction. To qualify for payroll deduction, you must be in the payroll system with a UCR appointment of 50% or greater (no fellowships, grants, or without salary appointments). Your appointment must be for a minimum of one year. 

  • Red Permit

    Red permits may be purchased at the Parking Services office.  Red permits are lot specific and sold based on lot availability. Waiting lists are maintained for high demand lots. You may add/remove yourself from waiting lists via our Parking Account Center by logging in with your NetID.

    Red permits are VALID:

    • In the Red lot designated on the permit
    • In Gold Lots 26, 30, 32, 50 
    • For up to 2 hours in non-reserved spaces of other Red or Blue lots one time per day
    • In Lot 25 Student Recreation Center 90 minute parking spaces one time per day
    • 6:00 PM-7:00 AM Monday-Friday and all day on weekends in all non-reserved spaces in Red, Blue and Gold permit lots

    Red permits are NOT VALID:

    • In Residence Hall, Campus Apartment or Oban Family Housing lots
    • In Pay-by-Space stalls
    • In Disabled or Medical stalls
    • In Service stalls
    • In any specially marked stalls
  • Blue Permit

  • "A" Carpool Permit

    The "A" Carpool permit is available to employees as follows:

    • All participants must first enroll and be pre-approved to participate in the Carpool Program prior to a permit being issued.
    • Requires a minimum of 4 employees to register as a 4+ carpool.
    • The permit cost will be split amongst all members of the carpool group. All participants must use payroll deduction as their method of payment.
    • The person living closest to campus must travel at least 50% of the ride with others in order to comply with AQMD rules.
    • Once approved, each member must come to TAPS to return their current parking permit, set‐up payroll deduction and register their vehicles. One person is considered the primary permit holder and all others in the group are eligible for a quarterly allotment of daily permits. The permit may be used by anyone in the group, but the vehicle must be registered to that permit.
    • The vehicle with the A carpool permit may park in any unreserved/unmarked space on campus.
    • It is the responsibility of the group to associate license plates to the permit online.

    "A" carpool permits are VALID:

    • In any non-reserved/unmarked space on campus

    • In Pay-by-Space stalls

    • In Service stalls

  • "A" Permit

    To purchase an "A" permit, an email must be sent to parking@ucr.edu from the department's Vice Chancellor or Dean authorizing the need for the "A" permit for the particular individual. An "A" permit is for use by employees who, based on job responsibilities/function, must park in locations which are distant from their assigned lot for extended periods of time beyond what a Red or Blue permit allows. Eligibility for an "A" permit is based on justified departmental needs, rather than individual convenience (e.g., an "A" permit is not intended to make attendance at meetings across campus more convenient). Individuals who are eligible to purchase an "X" permit may not purchase an "A" permit.

    An "A" permit can be paid for by the authorized individual or a department can pay the portion of the permit above the cost of the individual's Red or Blue permit. Please note that a department may not, at any time, purchase a parking permit with university funds (19900 funds) for use by any employee (faculty or staff) of the University.

    "A" permits are VALID:

    • In any non-reserved/unmarked space on campus

    • In Disabled or Medical stalls
    • In Service stalls

  • Emeritus/Retiree Permit

    For those faculty or staff who are retired and continuing services without compensation, Parking Services offers a free parking permit for use in any Gold parking lot or parking permits in Red and Blue lots for half price, based on availability.

    Retiree permits are non-transferrable and restricted to retirees only.

  • Medical Permit

    Faculty/Staff who need access to disabled or close proximity parking but do not have a California DMV Disabled Person Parking placard may apply for a temporary UCR medical permit by providing a letter from their physician stating the need for a medical parking permit for up to 60 days. Temporary medical parking permits are non-renewable. If you require medical parking past 60 days, you will need to get a California DMV Disabled Person Parking Placards or License Plate. Information on applying for a CA DMV placard/license plate may be found at https://www.dmv.ca.gov/portal/dmv/detail/pubs/brochures/fast_facts/ffvr07. Applications are submitted by mail and current DMV processing time is approximately 4 weeks.

    Temporary Medical permits are VALID:

    • In Red, Blue, Gold, Residence Hall, Campus Apartment and Oban Family Housing spaces
    • In Disabled spaces if displayed with a valid Disabled Person Parking Placard or DP license plate issued to you by the DMV

    Temporary Medical permits are NOT VALID:

    • In Pay-by-Space stalls
    • In Reserved stalls
    • In University Village disabled spaces
    • In Disabled spaces if the student does not have a Disabled Person Parking Placard or DP license plate issued to them by the DMV
