amirah dales

Amirah is originally from Pennsylvania, and has been a Los Angeles resident since her undergraduate studies at the University of Southern California (USC), where she graduated with a BS in Global Health, and minor in Classics. During her time at USC, Amirah completed multiple internships, and volunteered with the Global Health Network Uganda. Currently, Amirah works at the Los Angeles Regional Food Bank, as the Senior Programs Manager, overseeing a large grant to expand services to an additional 40,000 older adults in LA County.

austin kim

Austin is currently working for The Permanente Medical Group and manages the Ophthalmology and Dermatology Clinics in Redwood City, CA. He graduated from Embry-Riddle Aeronautical University in Daytona Beach, FL with a B.S. in Computational Mathematics. After undergraduate studies, Austin commissioned as an Officer in the United States Navy and completed 3 deployments over 5 years of service. After Active duty, Austin joined Kaiser Permanente as a Military Officer Transitioning Program Associate, which is a fellowship style program designed to integrate prior military officers into the organization.

Blaire Tribulski

Blaire Tribulski grew up in Murrieta, CA and completed her bachelor's degree in Health Education and Promotion with a Health Science emphasis from Utah State University in Northern Utah. She has since been working as a Nutritionist for the WIC program and has recently transitioned into a new role as a Health Educator for the Salt Lake County Health Department Substance Use Disorder Division. Since starting her career Blaire studied to obtain her Certified Health Education Specialist (CHES) certificate and has been pursuing professional development opportunities through her employer.

christina jung

She graduated from Princeton University magna cum laude with a BA in Ecology and Evolutionary Biology with a certificate in Neuroscience and African American Studies. She then moved to Maseru, Lesotho in Southern Africa after graduation to work with the Baylor Pediatric HIV/AIDS Initiative as a community outreach coordinator before returning to NYC for medical school at Albert Einstein in the Bronx.

clarence monteclaro

Dr. Clarence C. Monteclaro is a family physician of Filipino descent, raised in South Bay Los Angeles, and servers as Lead Physician at Wilmington Health Center, Ambulatory Care Network, DHS Los Angeles County. Imran Ahmed

Dr. Imran Ahmed is currently a Pediatric Dental Resident at UCLA-Westwood. He graduated Magna Cum Laude from University of California, Irvine with a BS in Neurobiology. Subsequently, he completed his dental school education and was awarded his Doctor of Dental Surgery from University of California, Los Angeles School of Dentistry.While in dental school, Dr. Imran was involved in research examining Community Oral Health Workers, focusing on their role in children's oral health, health literacy, and culturally competent interventions.

Jessica Arzola

Jessica Arzola is a Registered Nurse on a Cardiothoracic Surgical Unit at Ronald Reagan UCLA Medical Center. Her clinical practice majorly consists of helping patients prepare and recover from surgeries such as heart and lung transplantation, valve repairs, lobe resections, and esophagectomies. Jessica graduated from New York University with a BS in Nursing. In addition to being a Gates Millennium Scholar, Jessica was awarded the President’s Service Award upon graduation due to her numerous volunteer efforts with homeless and vulnerable populations within New York City.

Kemi Role

Kemi Role is currently the Director of Work Equity at a national workers’ rights organization. In her role, Kemi guides and leads efforts to remove barriers to employment, end employment discrimination and occupational segregation, address health and safety issues for workers, and build Black worker power; all within a framework of dismantling structural racism and centering Blackness. Kemi works closely with Black-led worker center partners across the country to co-develop policy and advocacy strategies that build Black worker power and amplify innovative strategies seeded within the community.

Magdalena Estrada

Magdalena Estrada is native from Guatemala. She immigrated to Los Angeles during the civil war in her country. Currently, Magdalena is a Health Education Manager at Eisner Health Community Center. Magdalena has been a perinatal health educator for over 20 years in Los Angeles and her community health education interest is maternity care as a reproductive justice matter. Magdalena graduated Cum Laude from Cal State Dominguez Hills with a Bachelor of Science in Community Health. She is a certified health education specialist (CHES), childbirth educator and board certified lactation consultant.

Megan received her B.A. in Environmental Studies/Biology with an emphasis in Agroecology from the University of California, Santa Cruz.

Michelle Katz

Sean McGivern

Dr. Sean McGivern hails from the great state of Iowa, where he worked as an associate dentist at the Iowa Pediatric Dental Center for two years. Dr. McGivern received both his undergraduate degree in biology and his doctorate of dental surgery from the University of Iowa. Dr. McGivern’s 15 years of research experience began at the Institute for Vision Research, a molecular ophthalmology laboratory at the University of Iowa. During dental school, his focus shifted from molecular genetics to public health and public policy.While at the National Institutes for Dental and Craniofacial Research in the Office of Science Policy and Analysis, Dr. McGivern studied access to care issues and provider participation in state Medicaid programs.

While in the Air Force, Dr. Trahan began sailing with the Special Olympics program and regularly enjoys volunteering with the program as well as sail racing.
