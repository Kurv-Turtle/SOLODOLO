Key Tools and Functions within Canvas

If you are new to using Canvas, you may appreciate some orientation to key Canvas tools and functions. 

  • Assignments: Use for students to upload submissions, from informal reflections to formal written assignments and projects to be viewed and assessed by the instructor.
  • Announcements: Use to send a quick message to your class. 
  • Discussions: Create space for students to engage with eachother and discuss class content - can also be a graded activity. 
  • Files: Use to post post key course documents, like the syllabus, readings, assignment sheets, etc. - can also be hidden from student view. 
  • Modules: Use to an organize course content for ease of student access. 
  • Pages: Use to create a Home Page, or create content for students to read or access that is not already created in a separate website or in a Word Document or other kind of document.

  • Canvas shells are automatically created for all College of the Canyons course sections, and are available to instructors who are are Online & Hybrid Instructor Certified, or Canvas Certified. See "Want to Teach Online?" for more information. 

  • To become Canvas Certified, you'll  need to complete a short, self-paced (and Flex-credit-eligible) online course, which will orient you to Canvas, and show you where to find, and how to use the tools and resources to build accessible course content, communicate with students, provide feedback on assessments, and create and grade assignments. See Course Guide for more information. Once you complete the course, you will be "Canvas Certified" and able to use Canvas to supplement  your face-to-face courses at College of the Canyons.

  • If you are Canvas Certified and do not see the course that you are scheduled to teach in Canvas: 

    • First, check if your course is listed under "All Courses" in Canvas. See How do I view all my Canvas courses as an instructor?
    • Please send an email to Chad Estrella, the COC Canvas Administrator: chad.estrella@canyons.edu. Please include your course name, section number and start date, along with any additional set-up requests (example, multiple sections in one course).
  • Canvas course shells will not be visible to students until the Instructor publishes the course. See How do I publish a course? for more information.
