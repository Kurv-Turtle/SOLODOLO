Service-Learning Fellows Program

The Service-Learning Fellows Program is a semester-long, cohort-based program designed to foster the development and institutionalization of service-learning courses and curricula, projects, and scholarly pursuits at Purdue, as well as strengthen collaborative efforts and outcomes in communities. The program is open to faculty (including non-tenure-track) and graduate student instructors seeking to implement service-learning pedagogy under today’s shifting circumstances. Up to $2,000 is available per project ($1,000 instructor discretionary funds and $1,000 community partner project). Please note, you do not need to have a partner identified as the service-learning staff will help develop these partnerships.

Fellowship Terms of Service

  • Collaboratively develop a plan for course and project implementation with community partner and Service-Learning Director
  • Participate in 9 cohort meetings
  • Develop and submit learning contract, learning outcomes & objectives, syllabus, student reflection assignment, curricular map, research impacts, reflections, final report
  • Apply for the Office of Engagement Societal Impact Fellows Program, Office of Engagement Scholarship of Engagement Fellows Program, engagement and service-learning grants, and/or produce a scholarly contribution, i.e., annual Purdue Engagement & Service-Learning Summit, co-author in Purdue Journal for Service-Learning and International Engagement
  • Contribute to the assessment of service-learning (instructor, student, community partner assessments)

Expected Use of Funds

  • No funds may be used for salaries or honoraria.
  • Funds may be used for travel needed to support the course/project.
  • Funds may be used for materials, supplies, publicity, printing, postage, digital platform technologies, etc. needed to support the course/project.
  • Funds may be used for professional development related to the project.
  • Funds may be used to support an intern or undergraduate/graduate research directly related to the project with approval by appropriate unit leadership.
  • Note: All activities and fund usage must be in compliance with the Protect Purdue guidelines and the standard operating procedures for Experiential Learning

Selection Process

A selection committee consisting of Office of Engagement representatives and former service-learning fellows selects the final award recipients. Announcement of award recipients occurs in April for fall cohorts and November for spring cohorts. The Office of Engagement manages and coordinates all functions and activities thereafter.

Featured Service-Learning Fellows

Dr. Bob Kenley

Dr. Kenley was a 2020-2021 Service-Learning Fellow. He teaches SYS 590: Service Learning and Systems Thinking at Purdue West Lafayette. During his fellowship, Dr. Kenley and his students partnered with HomesteadCS in Lafayette, IN. Together, they used systems thinking to devise a strategic plan for financial education classes that HomesteadCS will offer for college students.

Dr. Amanda Case

Dr. Case was a spring 2021 Service-Learning Fellow. She co-teaches EDPS 591/EDCI 590: Assessing Literacy Afterschool at Purdue West Lafayette. Dr. Case integrated her longstanding partnership with the Downtown Boxing Gym, an afterschool program in Detroit into her course. Students in the course created developmentally appropriate and culturally responsive literacy plans for middle- and high-school students.

Dr. Suining Ding

Dr. Suining Ding was a spring 2021 Service-Learning Fellow. She teaches INTR-D40001: Interior Design Studio I at Purdue Fort Wayne. As a fellow, Dr. Ding and her students partnered with Turnstone Center for Children and Adult with Disabilities in Fort Wayne, IN to design a long-term care facility based on the principles of universal design.
