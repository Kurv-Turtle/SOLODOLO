Committee on 

Academic Freedom 

The Committee on Academic Freedom was developed in order to have a more effective process for the Academic Senate to address Academic Freedom issues on campus. The members of the Committee on Academic Freedom are required to continuously educate themselves on the legal and professional elements of Committee on Academic Freedom. This committee will also be tasked with educating the campus community about academic freedom issues.

 

The Committee on Academic Freedome was formed by the Academic Senate President and the Academic Senate on September 17, 2020.

This committee was also formed per:

  • AP 4030 (Academic Freedom)
  • BP 4030 (Academic Freedom) 

These polices were adopted by the Academic Senate and the College Policy Council (CPC). Academic Freedom also lives within the realm of Title 5 and Ed Code requirements.

 

The following documents outline further academic freedom principles:

  • 1940 Statement of Principles on Academic Freedom and Tenure, adopted by the American Association of University Professors ("AAUP") and Academic Senate for California Community Colleges ("ASCCC")
  • "Academic Freedom and Tenure: A Faculty Perspective," adopted by Academic Senate in Spring 1998

The Committee on Academic Freedom meets 2nd and 4th Tuesday of each month during the regular semester from 3:00-4:30 p.m.
