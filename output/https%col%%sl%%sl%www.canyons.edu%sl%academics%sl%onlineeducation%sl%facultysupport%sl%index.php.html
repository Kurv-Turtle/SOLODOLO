Ways to Build Community Online

Providing frequent, predictable opportunities for students to interact with each other, engage with the learning content, and interact with the instructor, are among the essential components of an online learning environment that is rich with social connection, meaningful interaction, and satisfying learning. Explore techniques for creating connection between students, learning content, and the instructor.

Practice Trauma-Informed Pedagogy

As we prepare to welcome students back into our online classrooms this semester, remember that the emotions and concerns relating to the global pandemic may still be present for both students and instructors. You can build predictability and flexibility, create connection and warmth, and empower your students by incorporating trauma-informed teaching practices.

Make your Course Design Easier

The College of the Canyons Online Course Template resource shell includes a variety of ready-to-use course components for you to choose from, including an orientation module, example home pages and content module, and several powerful supplemental resources—all of which leverage effective pedagogy and accessibility principles.

Learn More

Explore 'Design Thinking' Workshop Series

Teaching is more than presenting a concept and asking students to demonstrate that they can repeat it back to us. Arguably, the most important goal of teaching is to spark that dynamic interplay of curiosity, exploration and engagement that results in learning. This series looks at the online learning environment from multiple perspectives, all aimed at giving you the ideas and tools to design effective instruction that engages your students and turns learning into success.
