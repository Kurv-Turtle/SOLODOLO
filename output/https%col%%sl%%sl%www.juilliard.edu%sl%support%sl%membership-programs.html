Major Gifts

Juilliard’s major gifts program celebrates individuals whose support advances the School’s mission of providing the highest caliber of artistic education for gifted musicians, dancers, and actors from around the world. The generosity of our major gifts patrons supports scholarships, educational outreach projects, entrepreneurial activities, Juilliard’s world-class faculty, and the wide range of performance and master class opportunities that are vital components of the artistic education of our students. IDNYC

Due to the ongoing pandemic, the IDNYC program is on pause for the foreseeable future. We will announce its return when our box office reopens and public performances resume. We look forward to welcoming you to Juilliard when it’s safe to do so.

Eligibility

Applicants must have received a valid IDNYC card in order to apply for a free membership at Juilliard. All IDNYC cardholders ages 18 and older may apply. If your IDNYC card has recently expired, you may renew your card online here.

Free IDNYC Juilliard memberships are not available to existing members or to individuals whose membership, or spouse's membership, has lapsed since January 1, 2015. If there is a question as to an individual's eligibility for a free membership, please call the Patrons’ Desk at (212) 769-7409.

NOTE: Free IDNYC Juilliard membership is limited to one per household. Please do not submit multiple applications per address, as only one will be processed. Households that have previously held an annual IDNYC complementary membership within the last five years will not be granted additional free membership/s.

What is IDNYC?
IDNYC is a government-issued photo identification card. IDNYC will secure the peace of mind and access to New York City services that come from having recognized identification. As an added benefit of enrollment, an IDNYC card entitles residents to complimentary benefits at various cultural institutions in New York City.

 

Who can obtain an IDNYC card?
All New York City residents. IDNYC benefits every city resident, including those in the most vulnerable communities: the homeless, youth, the elderly, undocumented immigrants, the formerly incarcerated, and others who may have difficulty obtaining other government-issued ID.

 

What is the cost of an IDNYC card?
All IDNYC cards are free.

 

What does Juilliard offer IDNYC card holders?
Juilliard is offering IDNYC card holders a free membership for one year at a level similar to Supporter ($100 value) in the Juilliard Association. Please note, however, that Juilliard’s offer of a free membership is limited to one per household; there are no exceptions.

 

What is included in a Juilliard IDNYC membership?

  • Digital subscription to our monthly member e-newsletter, Juilliard Journal e-newsletter, and emails featuring information about 700+ annual performances, most of which are free
  • Personalized, digital membership card
  • 20% discount on tickets to select paid performances, while supply lasts
  • 10% discount at The Juilliard Store (excludes textbooks)
  • Invitation to the Evening Division open house to find out about upcoming continuing education courses

*Please note that membership benefits are subject to change. For the most current listing of benefits please visit our Membership Policy section on this page or call the Patrons’ Desk at (212) 769-7409.

 

How do I apply for my Juilliard IDNYC Individual membership with my IDNYC card?
New Yorkers who have an IDNYC identification card and have not been a Juilliard member in the past five years, may become a member by presenting their IDNYC identification card in person at the Juilliard box office (155 West 65th St., New York, NY 10023). Regular box office hours are Monday-Friday, 11am-6pm.

Events

2020-2021 Juilliard Membership Events

Events for our members provide behind-the-scenes access to the Juilliard training process. We hope you will become a member and join us for these curated events to bring you closer to the Juilliard experience. 2021-2022 events will be announced soon.

Past Events in 2020-2021


Inside Look: Virtual Juilliard
Join us for an inside look at how Juilliard has created virtual learning experiences for each student. You’ll hear from key staff members about how the fall semester will work in a panel discussion led by Kaylen Ratto Giannini, associate director of annual giving.

Fall Talk: A Conversation with the Deans and Artistic Leadership of Juilliard
Hear how artistic leaders are educating students and running rehearsals and performances now. Enjoy performance selections by Juilliard artists.

Inside Look: The Art of Conducting
Director of Conducting Studies, David Robertson, leads a demonstration on how gestures impact performance.

Juilliard Celebrates the Holidays
Join us for a festive student showcase and preview of our upcoming virtual performance season.

Harpsichord Hangout
Join Juilliard experts as they explore the harpsichord, a gem of early music. Harpsichord faculty member Peter Sykes and Benjamin Sosland, administrative director of Historical Performance, engage Juilliard415 alum David Belkovski in a discussion of this foundational baroque instrument, its repertoire, and its history.

Inside Look: Recital Review
Hundreds of Juilliard students perform recitals each year, but how do these performances come together? Join us for a live discussion of all things recital prep: repertoire selection, musician collaboration, rehearsing, and speaking from the stage.

Behind the Lens: Choreographers and Composers
See how our faculty guide students as they collaborate and develop new works for this beloved annual class

Operatic Encores: Tchaikovsky’s Eugene Onegin
Alumni and faculty discuss and reminisce about Juilliard Opera’s 2014 production of Eugene Onegin, in advance of an exclusive viewing.

Juilliard Jazz Pre-show Lecture
Members enjoy this pre-show lecture from Jazz faculty member Loren Schoenberg, who conducted Juilliard Jazz: The Music of Bill Challis, Don Redman, and Eddie Durham.

Policies

Membership

  • Juilliard Memberships may only be shared with one (1) other person within the same household.
  • Invitations to Juilliard Membership events are non-transferrable. Members are allowed to bring one (1) guest to Membership events, for a total of two (2) attendees. Requests for additional guests will be considered on a case-by-case basis, subject to availability.
  • Contributions received from Foundations or Donor Advised Funds (‘DAF’) cannot be used towards memberships that incur a more than incidental benefit. Because the original contribution made to the Foundation or DAF provided the maximum tax deduction, the Foundation or DAF cannot receive any benefits that would change the value of the tax deduction. However, the donor-advisor can receive benefits that have insubstantial or no fair market value. Waiving the benefits associated with a membership will ensure no benefits of fair market value are received. Contact your financial advisor with questions about how these policies may affect your giving through a Foundation or DAF.

Member Tickets

  • Juilliard Members may purchase tickets to Juilliard events through the online Juilliard Ticket Portal or in person at the Juilliard Box Office. Juilliard Ovation members may contact the Patrons’ Desk to process ticket orders. Juilliard Association members may contact the Patrons’ Desk for ticket support.
  • For most Juilliard ticketed events, qualifying members may purchase up to two (2) tickets per performance or production at the 50% membership discount. On occasion, qualifying members may purchase up to four (4) tickets per performance at the 50% membership discount, subject to availability.
  • Juilliard Members may not use their membership to purchase discounted tickets on behalf of non-members.
  • All ticket sales are final and no refunds will be available. In some cases, tickets may be exchanged for a different date of the same event within the same production run (i.e. exchange a New Dances ticket for a different evening within the New Dances series), subject to availability. Juilliard Members may do so in person at the Juilliard Box Office. Juilliard Ovation members may also call the Patrons’ Desk for assistance for this type of exchange.
  • If a member cannot attend a performance, tickets may be donated back to The Juilliard School. This may be done in person at the Juilliard Box Office or members may contact the Patrons’ Desk. There are no refunds.
  • All Juilliard audience members are expect to be courteous and exercise appropriate behavior when attending Juilliard events. Any audience member who is in violation of appropriate behavior may receive a warning from Juilliard before further action is taken.

Contact Us

If you have questions about your membership status, benefits, events, performance calendar, or would like to become a member or renew your membership, please contact us at the Patrons’ Desk. We love hearing from our members and are happy to help you make your experience at Juilliard positive and fulfilling. Please note, our normal business hours are Monday through Friday, 9am-5pm, though we are closed on Fridays during the summer.
