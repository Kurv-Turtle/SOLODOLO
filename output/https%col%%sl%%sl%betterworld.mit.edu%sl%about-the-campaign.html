      Scholarships

      Scholarships—the foundation of MIT’s commitment to need-blind undergraduate admission—are a vital component of the Institute’s ability to provide a full range of support for students who have earned their place at MIT. Each financial promise to an outstanding undergraduate made during the Campaign for a Better World introduces a new voice into the MIT community of problem solvers and change makers, simultaneously enriching the life of the recipient and the campus.

      Unrestricted Giving

      During the Campaign, thousands of alumni and friends signaled their confidence in MIT by giving unrestricted funds—critical resources that can be directed where they are most needed. Such gifts helped MIT rapidly respond to the daunting challenges of Covid-19, stand firm in its longstanding commitment to undergraduate financial aid, and advance an environment of academic excellence and bold innovation.

      Working Together for the Future

      One of MIT’s greatest strengths is its ability to reach across disciplines in pursuit of a common goal. The Institute’s five schools and the MIT Stephen A. Schwarzman College of Computing are working together to address critical challenges and create positive change in the world.
      “To understand how design can solve critical problems, we needed a place where we could incubate innovations for the built environment. This Campaign has helped us do just that. The Metropolitan Storage Warehouse is on track to become the hub for design at MIT and in the world.”

      Hashim Sarkis, Dean, MIT School of Architecture and Planning; Professor of Architecture and Urban Planning

      Dean's Message Read more
      “When Covid-19 hit, we did what MIT engineers do. We rose to the occasion, united by a shared sense of purpose. I couldn’t be prouder of our entire community. When I say community, I put our donors right in the inner core. Philanthropic support was instrumental in enabling us to rapidly deploy solutions in record time.”

      Anantha P. Chandrakasan, Dean, MIT School of Engineering; Vannevar Bush Professor of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science

      Dean's Message Read more
      “Among all our recent projects, there is one that stands out that would have been impossible without the support of the Campaign: a state-of-the-art home for music that reflects the extraordinary significance of music to MIT students, to the Institute’s mission, and to our campus life.”

      Melissa Nobles, Chancellor; Professor of Political Science; Dean, MIT School of Humanities, Arts, and Social Sciences, 2015–2021

      Dean's Message Read more
      “Part of the story of science is that it takes an incredible amount of patience. Once you ask a question that you think is important and meaningful, it can take a very long time to find the answers. … Our friends and donors in the School of Science are investing in time to make a better world, and that is an immeasurable gift.”

      Nergis Mavalvala PhD ’97, Dean, MIT School of Science; Curtis and Kathleen Marble Professor of Astrophysics

      Dean's Message Read more
      “A brilliant person sitting by a fireplace with a yellow pad of paper writing down a brilliant idea is not going to change the world. Putting that idea into practice, realizing it, showing that it matters, showing that it works, proving that the world needs that idea—that is what is required to invent the future that we need.”

      David Schmittlein, John C Head III Dean, MIT Sloan School of Management; Professor of Marketing

      Dean's Message Read more
      “Across disciplines, we’re seeking to solve problems that matter to real people in the actual world. We’re creating computing bilinguals, experts in computer science and machine learning who … deeply understand another domain where computing can lead to dazzling advances, because you need both sets of expertise to solve the world’s problems at speed and at scale.”

      Daniel P. Huttenlocher SM ’84, PhD ’88, Dean, MIT Stephen A. Schwarzman College of Computing; Henry Ellis Warren (1894) Professor of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science
