Student Password Policy

Based on CSU security best practices, the campus password expiration guideline requires that all students change their campus supplied credential password annually. Note:  Your CSUF Portal and student email passwords are the same.

Changing Your Password

  1. Login to your Portal
  2. In the upper right corner click on your Profile icon
  3. Click on Settings
  4. Under Student Account Setting s click on Edit next to Change Portal Password
  5. Type in your current, new and confirm new passwords
  6. Click on Update
  7. Update your password for the campus WiFi on your devices.

Changing Your Security Questions

Setting up security questions will allow you to use the self-service password reset option if you forget your password.

  1. Login to your Portal
  2. In the upper right corner click on your Profile icon
  3. Click on Settings
  4. Under Student Account Setting s click on Edit next to Password Reset Security Questions
  5. Select  3 different questions and provide answers. Note: the word/pharse you use for your answer is not case sensitive
  6. Click on Update

Password Requirements

  • Between 12-20 characters
  • Contain at least 3 of the following criteria:
    • Uppercase letters
    • Lowercase letters
    • Numbers
    • Special Characters

These are the allowed characters:

! @ $ % & * ( ) , . - _ +

  • No part of your name can be used
  • You cannot use your last 24 campus passwords

Reset Your Password Online

Unable to reset your password online because you forgot your security questions?

You can:

  • Call the Student IT Help Desk at (657) 278-8888
  • Walk-in: Student Genius Center, Pollak Library North - 1st Floor

You will be required to verify ID via Zoom (for phone support) AND show a valid photo ID such as driver's license, state ID, or passport (for phone and walk-in support).

Passwords will not be reset over email or chat
