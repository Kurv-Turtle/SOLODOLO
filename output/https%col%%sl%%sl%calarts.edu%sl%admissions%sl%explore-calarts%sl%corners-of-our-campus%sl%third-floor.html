Third Floor

The third floor houses most of the large exhibition and performance spaces at CalArts, though work is created and exhibited across the entire campus. Also on this floor is Steve's Café, our on-campus cafeteria dining space. The Main Gallery is the figurative center of campus and home to many events, such as our annual Halloween Party.

Blue Wall

Blue Wall

Institute

CalArts' distinctive Blue Wall is the unofficial face of the campus, and greets visitors, faculty, staff, and students on their way into campus.

Dizzy Gillespie Digital Recording Studio (DRS)

Dizzy Gillespie Digital Recording Studio (DRS)

•School of Music

The Dizzy Gillespie Digital Recording Studio (DRS) is a suite of connected rooms that function as a 48-channel, professional studio for recording, editing, mixing, and mastering. The control room and live room are acoustically treated and tuned for optimal sound isolation.

Equity and Diversity

Equity and Diversity

Institute

CalArts is committed to a diverse and equitable environment in the workplace and within the classrooms, studios, and beyond. Issues of access, equity, and inclusion are indispensable to the vitality of CalArts, where the community constantly strives to deepen intercultural awareness and provide robust support mechanisms for students from increasingly varied cultural and ethnic backgrounds.

Learn More

Financial Aid Office

Financial Aid Office

Institute

The Financial Aid Office provides financial assistance, resources, and information, fostering the educational goals of CalArts students by focusing on financial strategies that help promote access, affordability, and persistence toward completing their degree program as well as succeeding beyond our campus.

Learn More

Galleries D300 & D301

Galleries D300 & D301

•

School of Art

D300 and D301 are two white cube galleries available to students in the School of Art for exhibitions and performances.

Jory Prum Experimental Sound Studio

Jory Prum Experimental Sound Studio

•School of Music

The Jory Prum Experimental Sound Studio has equipment for computer-based composition, audio recording, sound synthesis, sound design, and film scoring. It is the primary studio for music students who work with processed and sample-based sound, and serves as a classroom for composition, sound-synthesis, and film scoring.

Learn More

L Shape Gallery

L Shape Gallery

•

School of Art

One of the seven main galleries available for exhibitions and performances in the School of Art, the L-Shape Gallery is open and heavily trafficked as it is on the main level by the café.

Main Gallery

Main Gallery

Institute

The Main Gallery is the central hub of the Institute, the figurative center of campus. Exhibitions, performances, the Halloween Party, internship and career fairs, and much more take place in this large space at the heart of campus.

Learn More

Management Offices

Management Offices

•School of Theater

Similar to the Design Studio, the Management Offices are work areas where the students from the creative producing, management, and technical direction métiers have individual workspaces in a shared environment.

MOD Theater

MOD Theater

•School of Theater

The Walt Disney Modular Theater is a completely modular theater space with an overall audience and playing area of 4,480 square feet. Comprised of 4’x4’ sections which can be raised and lowered in increments, the inner walls are also composed of 340 4’x4’ removable panels, allowing for infinite reconfiguration and limitless design possibilities.

Modular Synth Studio

Modular Synth Studio

•School of Music

The Modular Synth Studio is home to the music school’s rare “Black Serge” analog modular synthesizer, as well as a 12-unit hybrid Blacet/Eurorack modular synthesizer, an Oberheim Synthesizer Expander Module, a Moog Sub 37 Analog Synthesizer, and a rare Rob Hordijk Blippo Box.

Learn More

Music Office

Music Office

•School of Music

The Music Office is the administrative hub of the entire School of Music. Here is where you can find faculty offices, the dean's office, and support staff.

Noon Concerts at the Main Gallery

Noon Concerts at the Main Gallery

•School of Music

Noon Concerts in the Main Gallery feature varied and diverse performance activities from The Herb Alpert School of Music across all specializations. These concerts are very popular and well attended by those on campus and in the wider community.

Percussion Room

Percussion Room

•School of Music

This teaching/performance space provides an outstanding venue for small- and medium-sized ensembles, and is home to dozens of percussion instruments and modular setups for percussion rehearsals and performances of all kinds.

Roy O. Disney Concert Hall (ROD)

Roy O. Disney Concert Hall (ROD)

•School of Music

The Roy O. Disney Concert Hall (ROD) has adaptable acoustics, lighting, and sound and supports recitals, concerts, and multimedia and experimental presentations. The space also doubles as a live recording room. A livestream of ROD concerts is viewable through the link below.

Learn More

Stevenson Blanche Gallery

Stevenson Blanche Gallery

•

School of Art

Stevenson Blanche is a student-led exhibition space, managed by a committee of Art School BFA students. Students may invite other schools within CalArts for collaborative exhibitions, concerts, and performances.

Steve's Cafe

Steve's Cafe

Institute

Steve's Café serves a wide range of food choices, including vegan and vegetarian options. Seating ranges from intimate spaces to group tables—all intended to make the café a hub for the entire campus.

The Sharon Disney Lund Dance Theater

The Sharon Disney Lund Dance Theater

•School of Dance

The Sharon Disney Lund Dance Theater is a 50x70-foot space used exclusively for dance performances, rehearsals and classes. It features state-of-the-art wall-to-wall L'Air sprung flooring, covered with seamless black Lonstage. Retractable theater seating allows for many different staging configurations. Seating capacity is usually 100 to 125 persons, with a maximum capacity of 250.

Wave Cave

Wave Cave

•School of Music

The WaveCave is a venue for music students to develop and exhibit interactive sound installations, video installations, interactive workshops, listening sessions, and all forms of sound art and sculpture. The WaveCave installation blog can be viewed through the link below.
