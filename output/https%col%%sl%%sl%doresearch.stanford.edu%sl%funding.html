Funding

The process of finding funding is incumbent upon the research project team. There are resources to help you identify and pursue funding opportunities from a variety of sponsors. Following are major funding resources, including several that are managed by VPDoR.

 

On This Page

    Broad Funding Search

    The Vice Provost and Dean of Research Office and Stanford Libraries maintain a comprehensive Funding Resources Search database, where you can find funding opportunities, awarded grants, and news. DeepResearch (formerly known as XSearch) allows you to search across multiple databases at once (Pivot, Foundation Directory, Stanford Seed Funding, Grants.gov). Log in using your Stanford email address.

    DeepResearch

    Alternatively, visit individual databases for more advanced search functions.

    Pivot

    Pivot is a funding opportunities database that helps you find grant notices in medicine, humanities, social sciences, engineering and science from federal and private sources. Stanford University maintains an institutional subscription, making this service freely available to the Stanford community. See also our DoResearch page on how to use Pivot.

    Grants.gov

    Outside of Stanford, grants.gov is a publicly accessible database that houses information on over 1,000 grant programs and vets grant applications for 26 federal grant-making agencies.

    Grants.ca.gov

    Grants.ca.gov is managed and hosted by the California State Library and  provides a centralized location to find state grant opportunities. State agencies are required to register each of their grants in the portal prior to the beginning of the solicitation or award process.

    Internal Funding Opportunities

    Stanford Seed Funding is a site for faculty to find and apply for internal funding opportunities. This service is part of a Dean of Research initiative to reduce the administrative burden incurred by faculty and their teams, and to afford them more time for our shared core mission of research and teaching. Seed Funding includes all internal research funding whose purpose is to sponsor risky new ideas that might not get funding from traditional sources, or to launch careers for researchers who have yet to win a federal grant.

    Stanford Seed Funding

    Limited  Submissions

    The Stanford Research Development Office and the SoM Research Management Group manage the university-wide Limited Submissions Programs, a collection of funding opportunities with a limitation in the number of applicants allowed per institution. They promote these limited opportunities to the Stanford research community and ask interested parties to apply via our application portal powered by InfoReady.

    Answers to commonly asked questions regarding the limited submission process at Stanford University are available here.

    Contact us at limitedsubmissions@stanford.edu.

    Foundation Relations  

    If you're working with a non-profit foundation, find out if that sponsor you are interested in applying to is on the list of managed foundations by the University Corporate and Foundation Relations Office.

    Links to current requests for proposals and funding search tools curated by University and Corporate Foundations are available here.

    SOLO

    Stanford Research Development Office

    The Stanford Research Development Office (RDO) is a unit under VPDoR that aims to strengthen collaborative or strategic research and scholarly activities by helping to set teams up for funding success through project-opportunity alignment, early preparation, and proposal development support.

    RDO supports research teams from across the University, with an emphasis on complex or strategic proposals. This often includes large, multi-PI, multi-disciplinary proposals, but can also apply to other projects depending on the discipline or specific situation.

    RDO’s goal is to enhance competitiveness of proposals through grantsmanship while reducing the burden on PIs. Examples of support might include finding the right fit between project and sponsor, providing guidance for strategic preparation and concept development, coordinating projects, strengthening the responsiveness of grant applications, writing and editing proposals, and enhancing the structure and presentation of all proposal components. Our services are optional, and free to PIs. In addition, we manage the Limited Submission Program for the University, in collaboration with the School of Medicine.

    Within the Stanford research lifecycle, RDO offers (pre-)pre-award support with expertise in grantsmanship and experience with various sponsors (mostly federal, or opportunities with open calls). In this way RDO complements other services such as budget development by local research administration support, compliance review and submission by OSR/RMG, support for proposals to foundations by UCFR and to industry sponsors by Industrial Contracts Office.
