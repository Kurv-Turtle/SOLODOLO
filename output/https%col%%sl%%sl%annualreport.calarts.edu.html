From the President

Dear Friends,

I am pleased to present the return of an important practice -- the publication of an annual report. The 2020-2021 CalArts Annual Report is an opportunity to celebrate and honor our community of artists by recognizing the contributions and accomplishments that so many have made throughout the past academic year. As it was for so many, it was a challenging year for the CalArts Community, but as this report illustrates, through the hard work of the many faculty and staff, and the dedication of our students, we continued to carry out our mission to enable young artists to transform themselves, each other, and the world, through what they create.

As happened in 1970-1971 at the beginning of CalArts’ time in Valencia, the beginning of the pandemic delayed our ability to mark the 50th anniversary of CalArts as planned -- but again only by a year. Passing the mid-century mark is a significant achievement and a cause for celebration. But we’re using this opportunity to not only honor our storied past but also to look forward to our future. In 2018 the Institute embarked on an ambitious Strategic Visioning Project, engaging the entire CalArts community, including faculty, staff, students, trustees, alumnx, and friends, in a dialogue about the Institute’s past and present. Our goal was to envision what CalArts might look like over its next five decades. In March 2020, just before the pandemic took hold of Los Angeles, the Board of Trustees approved and adopted the 2020 Strategic Framework that was a product of the visioning of the two years before it.

With this Strategic Framework, our community has committed to ensuring that CalArts remains bold and sustainable; grounded in openness, experimentation, critical engagement, and creative freedom. Over our next fifty years, CalArts will strive to live up to these aspirational values. In this report, you will see some examples of how we are already beginning to do this. But there is much more work ahead of us, work we must do together, and for that we need the commitment of our entire community of artists.

Thank you for your ongoing support and belief in our mission -- one that focuses on artists, and not just art. Your efforts on behalf of our students and the work that we do is greatly appreciated, and I look forward to seeing what we will do together in the years ahead. Learn more about our history, mission, values, and leadership Ravi S. Rajan
President
