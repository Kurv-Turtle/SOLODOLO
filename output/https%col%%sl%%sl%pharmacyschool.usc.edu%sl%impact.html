Impact

For more than a century, the USC School of Pharmacy has been a beacon of innovation and excellence. As the only school that spans the entire pharmaceutical spectrum of discovery and development to clinical care, regulation, and policy and economics, the beneficial impact of our work is global.

STAR

Life-Changing Research

The USC Science Technology and Research (STAR) program, based at the USC School of Pharmacy for more than three decades, brings together USC scientists with students at nearby Francisco Bravo Medical Magnet High School. STAR students learn about science and math by conducting research that matters with USC experts.

Global

Improving the World

For nearly three decades, we have maintained international partnerships that now extend throughout Asia, the European Union, Australia, South America and Africa. With our location in Los Angeles, we are uniquely positioned as a bridge between East and West. From joint scientific meetings and research collaborations to student exchanges and internships to training programs for foreign colleagues and service-learning missions, the school’s reach and influence are global.

Discovery
DISCOVERY

Creating New Knowledge

In our laboratories, faculty and students continuously expand our understanding of the mechanisms of disease development and progression as they uncover breakthrough therapeutics and approaches to cure and treat a host of maladies. From cancer and infectious disease to autoimmune and cardiovascular conditions and from neurodegeneration to substance use, our researchers produce groundbreaking advances and garner millions of dollars of funding from the National Institutes of Health and other government and private funders.

Care
Care

Building Healthy Communities

In addition to serving patients in a wide variety of clinical settings—including more than 50 annual health fairs—we are the only school in the nation that owns and operates five pharmacies. More than half of L.A.’s first million doses of COVID-19 vaccines were delivered at sites we administered. Our faculty members are national leaders in promoting pharmacy practice in safety-net clinics for the homeless and uninsured, and in eliminating pharmacy deserts. Our team of regulatory scientists is helping safely speed lifesaving medications and devices to those who need them, while our health economists provide evidence-based research that is shaping advances in health policy nationally and globally.

Fotonovelas

Community Health Outreach

The popular fotonovela series combines health information with dramatic storytelling and photography. More than half a million booklets, published in English and Spanish, have been distributed at safety-net clinics, health fairs, pharmacies and other locations to date, providing information to at-risk populations in a culturally sensitive way.
