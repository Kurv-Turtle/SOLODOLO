Types of International Applicants

 

What Kind of International Student Am I?
Full-Time Student
(from Outside the U.S.)

An applicant outside the U.S. who wishes to study in-person and full-time at College of the Canyons on an F-1 (student) visa, and who does not yet have an active F-1 visa.
Full-Time Transfer Student
(from Inside the U.S.)

An international student who wishes to study full-time at College of the Canyons on an F-1 (student) visa, who is currently studying at another DHS-approved school in the United States, and who is applying from inside the United States.
Part-Time Student for Concurrent Enrollment
(from Inside the U.S.)

An F-1 student who is enrolled at another DHS-approved and SEVP-certified school in the United States under their F-1 sponsership and wishes to concurrently enroll part-time at College of the Canyons.
Part-Time F-2 Student
(from Inside the U.S.)

An F-2 dependent of an F-1 student, and who is applying from inside the United States.

NOTE: F2 sponsorship is restricted to persons that qualify to attend the College.

Online-Only Student from Home Country
(from Outside the U.S.)

Applicants who will be studying online only at COC from outside the U.S.

NOTE: The ONLY items required from online-only students outside the U.S. are the ISP Application with Photo and Passport, Proof of Secondary Education, and Proof of English Proficiency.

English Language Requirement

The English language tests and minimum scores listed in the table below are accepted by College of the Canyons for international applicants. College of the Canyons recommends Duolingo as an English Language test.

Language Training: Students attend the Intensive English Language Program (IELP)
Pathways: Students take English as a Second Language classes (ESL)
Associates: Students take college level English classes (English 101, English 103, Etc.)

Test Name  Language Training Pathways Associates
Duolingo 55-89 90-115 116 -160
TOEFL iBT 45-49 iBT 50-75 iBT 76-120
IELTS 5.0 5.5-6.0 6.5-9.0
EIKEN 5-Pre 2 2-2A Pre 1-1
EF SETPlus 31-49 50-75 76-100

 

Alternative proof of English language proficiency includes:

  • Proof of citizenship (birth certificate, passport, etc.) from a country that uses English as a primary language in its educational system.
  • Transcripts from a U.S. college or university verifying a minimum of one year attendance and completion of General Education classes with a GPA of 2.0 or above.
  • Transcripts from a college or university that provides instruction in English verifying a minimum of one year attendance and completion of General Education classes with a GPA of 2.0 or above.
  • Graduation certification or transcripts verifying graduation from a U.S. high school.

EFSET Scores:

Click here to read the interpretation and admittance guide for the EFSET test. Scores are based on the Common European Framework of Reference for Languages (CEFR) language proficiency levels.
