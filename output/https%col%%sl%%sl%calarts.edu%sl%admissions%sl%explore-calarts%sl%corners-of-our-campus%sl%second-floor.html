Second Floor

Almost as busy as the third floor, the second floor of CalArts houses a mix of production and fabrication spaces, as well as places used by every student like the Library, the Writing Center, the Bijou Cinema, the Student Union, the CalArts Store, and Tatum Lounge/Coffee Bar.

Accounting

Institute

The Accounting Office helps students and parents with tuition statements, related paperwork, and options for payments, in addition to other student account services.

Learn More

African Drum Room

African Drum Room

•School of Music

This room contains an extensive instrument and costume collection for intensive study of polyrhythmic music and dance from Africa's Ghanaian region. Visiting artists teach repertoires from many African cultures. Students are immersed in performance, theory, transcription, and analysis and gain practical skills for maintaining, repairing, and tuning instruments.

Alumnx/Advancement Mural

Alumnx/Advancement Mural

Institute

This mural in and near the Office of Alumnx and Family Engagement is titled Black Magic, Blessed Children. It is made with acrylic paint on Colombian maple. Alumnus Cash-Cooper dedicated the piece in memory of Peggy Cooper Cafritz. The mural is inspired by many pantheons and mythological origin stories of a variety of cultures and traditions, depicting the artist's interpretation of the origin story, magic, and community of CalArts.

School of Art

The Art Office is the administrative hub of the entire School of Art. Here is where you can find faculty offices, the dean's office, and support staff.

Bijou Movie Theater

Bijou Movie Theater

•School of Film/Video

The Bijou is CalArts' on-campus cinema. Projection capability includes digital cinema, 35 mm, and 16 mm film. It is also used to host visiting artist, filmmaker, and alumnx screenings and presentations, and the end-of-year Bijou Film Festival where film students present their work during a weeklong film festival.

The CalArts Store sells apparel, buttons, and other swag, as well as basic sundries and art and class supplies. CalArts T-shirts, sweatshirts, and more are available through the online store.

Center for Life and Work

Center for Life and Work

Institute

The Center for Life and Work at CalArts provides workshops, job search counseling, career information sessions, recruitment fairs, an internship program, and assists students with beginning a life and career in the arts.

Character Animation Cubicles

Character Animation Cubicles

•School of Film/Video

Cube/studio spaces reserved for Character Animation students.

Coffeehouse Theater

Coffeehouse Theater

•School of Theater

The Coffeehouse Theater is used as a performance space for workshop productions, student generated work, and classroom exploration. Also used as a gathering space for community events, the theater is approximately 1,750 square feet, and has a pipe grid, a control booth, a dimming system, a lighting/sound inventory, and a piano.

Community Arts Partnership (CAP) Office

Community Arts Partnership (CAP) Office

Institute

The award-winning CalArts Community Arts Partnership (CAP), a co-curricular program of CalArts, offers free after-school and school-based arts programs for youth ages 4-18 in every discipline taught at the Institute. Enrolled students at CalArts are eligible to apply to teach for CAP as an on-campus job.

Learn More

Dance Dressing Rooms

Dance Dressing Rooms

•School of Dance

The School has two student dressing rooms with keycard access, one men’s and one women’s. Each room has 12 lighted mirror stations, two showers, sinks, bathroom stalls, and costume areas.

Dance Studios

Dance Studios

•School of Dance

There are four full-time dance studios and a fifth studio available for composition classes. The three primary studios are large and feature windows that admit natural light, and are equipped with mirrored walls, fixed and portable barres, a piano, sound and video playback equipment, and state-of-the-art L'Air sprung floors covered in seamless Lonstage.

Design Studio

Design Studio

•School of Theater

A collective environment housing the workspaces for the entire design cohort during their time in the program. Students have their own desks and shared workspaces in close proximity to their peers, as well as space for storage and printing.

Film Services Library

Film Services Library

•School of Film/Video

Film and Image Services is the media center of the library. They are responsible for the acquisition, access, management, and preservation of the moving image and digital still image collections. There are private and group viewing stations available. Film and Image Services also manages and schedules the Bijou Theater.

Learn More

Film/Video Office

Film/Video Office

•School of Film/Video

The School of Film/Video Office houses faculty offices, the dean's office, administrative operations, and the Equipment Cage. It also has a nice lounge area where you can often find James Benning working on his laptop from the couch.

Instrument Collection

Instrument Collection

•School of Music

CalArts maintains a collection featuring 50 pianos, two harpsichords, an organ, a celesta, and two two harps; various string, woodwind, brass, and early European instruments; numerous orchestral and non-traditional percussion instruments; Balinese and Javanese gamelans; African drum ensembles; and sarods, sitars, tablas, and other Indian instruments.

KCIA Radio

KCIA Radio

Institute

KCIA Radio is an interdisciplinary, completely student-run radio station. KCIA aims to amplify the voice of the CalArts community through the creation of diverse on-air content, live events, and engagement with the musical landscape of the greater Los Angeles area.

Library

Office of Campus Life (Clubs and Organizations)

Office of Campus Life (Clubs and Organizations)

Institute

CalArts has an expansive and ever-changing list of student clubs, hosted through our Office of Student Life. Some examples include: CalArts Comedy, Black Arts Collective, Contact Improv Club, Film Tonight, Game Makers, Hardware Hacking Club, Mi Gente (Latin Student Union), Queer Arts Collective, Soccer Club, and much more! Students can (and do) start their own clubs based on their interests.

Office of Marketing and Communications

Office of Student Experience

Office of Student Experience

Institute

Student Experience provides a variety of student support and advocacy services. The staff is dedicated to helping students deal with challenges and problems in all aspects of their lives.

Pilates Studio

Pilates Studio

•School of Dance

The fully carpeted Pilates Studio measures 39x23 feet. It has a mirrored wall and includes the following equipment: five Pilates studio reformers, two trapeze tables, one "whale," four tumbling mats, a Wunda Chair, a Ladder Barrel, and a PaceMaster Pro Plus Treadmill.

Practice Room(s)

Practice Room(s)

•School of Music

The Herb Alpert School of Music has more than 25 practice rooms, many with pianos, including designated rooms for piano and percussion majors.

Scene Shop & Welding Lab

Scene Shop & Welding Lab

•School of Theater

The School of Theater maintains a dedicated 24/7 scene shop that consists of a wood shop, a metal shop, a CNC room, an OSHA-approved spray booth and finishing area, material storage areas, and assembly areas. The scene shop is staffed by a full-time scene shop manager with faculty overseeing student projects/production on a daily basis.

Student Health Center

Student Health Center

Institute

CalArts is dedicated to supporting the entire student body, and offers a variety of health and recreation services to help each and every person thrive, including an on-site nurse, counselors to support mental health, group sports, and camping equipment.

Learn More

Student Union

Student Union

Institute

The Union functions to unite the student body in order to foster a fully interdisciplinary, progressive, and creative environment. The Union is headed by elected representatives from each of the six schools, as well as leaders of other student groups.

Super Shop

Super Shop

•

School of Art

The Super Shop is an Institute-wide resource that provides facilities and technical instruction for making three-dimensional artwork. It is a sculpture-driven workspace with full woodworking, metal fabrication, ceramics, sandblasting, and spraying facilities. Basic hand tools are available for checkout.

Tabla Room

Tabla Room

•School of Music

Focusing heavily on vocalization and syllable recitation, students study North Indian classical music holistically, building advanced technical skills with ragas, talas, sruti, and microtones. Students are immersed in performance, theory, transcription, and analysis to gain practical skills for maintaining, repairing, and tuning instruments.

Tatum

Tatum

Institute

Tatum Lounge is an informal gathering place and coffee house, featuring a daytime coffee bar—with snacks and pastries—staffed by students. Students can lounge on sofas and chat with friends, listen to the baristas' playlists, hear a live reading, or view an art exhibition.

The Cage

The Cage

•School of Film/Video

The Equipment Cage contains all the portable production equipment available in the School of Film/Video, which includes everything from 16 mm film to 4K digital cinema. Equipment is available for student use during the academic year, including winter and spring breaks. Equipment list available here.

Learn More

The Palace

The Palace

•School of Film/Video

The Palace is a large, multi-purpose classroom used for larger classes, figure drawing sessions, Character Animation program meetings, Friday night guest lectures, screenings, and other special events.

Learn More

Writing Center

Writing Center

Institute

The Writing Center, located in the Library, is a place where students can meet one-on-one with trained writing tutors to work on any writing-related task they face—from academic papers to artist statements, grant applications to resumes.
