Tuesday's Synopsis

NOTE: SPURIOUS TEMPERATURE/HUMIDITY SENSOR READINGS BECAME CHRONIC BY THE MEMORIAL DAY WEEKEND. THE SENSORS WERE CLEANED AND WIRING ADJUSTED, WHICH APPEARED TO CORRECT THE PROBLEM. HOWEVER, FURTHER SPURIOUS READINGS ARE PROBABLE TO RETURN IN THE COMING WEEKS, BASED ON PAST ADJUSTMENTS.

A large, upper level trough of low pressure exists over the northern Rocky Mountain states. The southern portion of this trough helped bring cooler weather to well inland areas of southern California. High pressure will build into the state for a couple of days (starting tonight), and warmer weather is anticipated. Temperatures in well inland areas may reach levels that occurred a few days ago. Depending on whether a shallow marine layer persists, the coastal plain may or may not get as warm as most computer model forecasts show (valid for tomorrow and Thursday). Last week, the models overpredicted how much warming would take place nearer the coast (marine layer didn't shrink as much as predicted).

A new, upper level trough will approach the state from the west toward the end of the week. The trough should induce stronger on-shore flow as it reaches the coast over the weekend. Most of the models predict an episode of "June Gloom" weather west of the mountains on Saturday (possibly Sunday too). Temperatures should be similar to what occurred the last couple of days. However, there probably won't be "surprise" drizzle/light rain in places as happened yesterday (oddly, barely measurable rain fell in spots away from the mountains).

Another warming cycle should begin next week. Model agreement is only fair and only on the general trend of warmer weather (nothing too strong west of the mountains...predicted centers of high pressure aloft should remain well to the east and west of the state through next week). As long as a shallow but defined marine layer persists, there should be no significant warming trend for the coastal plain. Since some of the models have repeatedly erred in purging the marine layer, and I'm uncertain whether this will happen next week. At some point, it will (does so occasionally every warm season).

Next issued forecast/synopsis should be on Tuesday, 21 June. With summer approaching, I am switching to once a week issued forecasts. There may be some weather events that warrant a couple of forecasts in a week, but that'll be announced in advance.
