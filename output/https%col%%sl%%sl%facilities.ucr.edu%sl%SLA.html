Facilities Services provides the maintenance, operation, repair and light alteration to UCR classrooms, offices, research labs, on-campus housing communities, landscape and athletic facilities. Our service level guides outline services provided to campus by Facilities Services to address the campus's operational needs while also addressing regulatory or policy mandates.

Service Level Priorities

Facilities Services uses service levels to help our crews prioritize work and for reporting of different classes of work. The services levels below, described as "SL 1 - 6" are commonly used to prioritize work for Maintenance Services. We make sure each service level is assigned the appropriate service level based on the level of urgency of the work order. 

SL1

 Urgent 


Emergency work posing a risk to life, safety, and property

FS will respond within 0-4 hours 


 

Examples of an SL1 are: 

  • Entry door damage
  • Elevator entrapment
  • Excess water flow / slip hazard
  • Flood / fire / gas / live-exposed electrical
  • Graffiti removal
  • Water outage in a dining facility
     

SL2
Immediate 


Work required to mitigate imminent threats to research, instruction or safety

FS will respond within 2 days

Examples of an SL2 are: 

  • Cold room failure
  • Containment breaches
  • Dining services conveyor belt or dish machine repair
  • Elevator inoperable (no entrapment) 
  • Minor water leak from roof
  • Trip hazards
     

SL3

Essential


Work required to ensure continuity of general instruction / research mission

Facilities Services will respond within 10 days

Examples of an SL3 are: 

  • 25% of lights are out in a room
  • Broken seating in a classroom
  • Drippy faucet 
  • Interior door not closing properly
  • Pest control (Insects) 
  • Urinal not properly working

SL4

Normal


Maintenance and repair work required impacting campus operations

Facilities Services will respond within 35 days

Examples of an SL4 are: 

  • Card access to a building not working
  • Clean carpet / floor
  • Damage to built-in furniture
  • Event support
  • Furniture  move 
  • General repairs 

SL5

Multi-trade Minor Renovation


Multiple trade minor renovation under $50,000

Completed as labor resource allocations and funding permits; work is prioritized by Deans and Vice Chancellors quarterly 

Examples of an SL5 are: 

  • Facility modifications: move a door, install a work bench, etc. 
  • Install a fume hood 
  • Install a hydration station
  • Mount a TV and add electrical
  • New carpet and blinds installation
  • Painting a lab, office, hallway, etc.  

To learn more about the prioritization process, visit the R'Projects website. 

SL6

Single Trade Minor Renovation


Single trade minor renovation under $50,000

Completed within 60 business days (recognizing labor resource allocations and limits) 

Examples of an SL6 are: 

  • Add an electrical outlet 
  • Hang a whiteboard / picture
  • Installations
  • Metal / sign fabrication
  • Installation of a security alarm
  • New key / alarm add request
  • New sign installation
  • Seismic bracing

SL7

HDHS (PSLA)

Maintenance work orders generated for Housing or Dining locations - Apartments and Residence Halls 

Initial response time is 24 hours from the request submission according to the PSLA

Examples of a Service Level 7 are: 

  • Electrical outlet not working
  • Damaged furniture 
  • Door not closing
  • Dripping faucet
  • Toilet malfunction
  • Water leak

SL8
Dining Services

Maintenance work orders generated for Dining locations

Initial response time is 24 hours from the request submission according to the PSLA

Examples of a Service Level 8 are: 

  • Low water temperatures 
  • Kitchen refrigerator not cooling
  • Dining facility doors not locking
  • Gas pilot needs relighting
  • Walk-in freezer running warm
  • Add/remove employee from safe access
