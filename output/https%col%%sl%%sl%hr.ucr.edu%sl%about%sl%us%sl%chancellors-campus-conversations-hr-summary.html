Strategies

In April, 2017, Human Resources (HR) participated in the Chancellor's Campus Conversations to discuss opportunities and challenges facing the UCR campus. This page provides an update on activity related to the key items which were identified during the roundtable discussions.

  1. Strengthen central HR functions, including support for onboarding and recruitment, better communication with units/departments, and automated systems.
  2. Increase staff retention and morale by offering more opportunities for professional development, internal promotion, and career advancement.
  3. Address staff workload, to ensure student and faculty needs are met.
  4. Provide pathways to hire UCR undergraduate and graduate students upon graduation.

Automated Systems

November
2020

The Human Resources Management System (HRMS) is a collaboration between Human Resources, Information Technology Solutions (ITS) and our HR system users as part of an ongoing effort to provide the campus with updated HR technology. Scheduled for implementation in 2020, the HRMS will centralize UCR developed HR tools into a single application and contain the following modules:

  1. Job Builder: Expands Career Tracks to incorporate the Job Description System (JDS) resulting in all job descriptions being created and maintained in a single application.
  2. Review: Replaces iReview actions - Equity, Reclass and Reclass Salary Review.
  3. Recruitment: Replaces a subset of existing iRecruit functionality.
  4. Job Code Information: Replaces UCR Title and Pay Plan (TPP) containing job code attribute information.

All modules are being developed to provide similar navigation and user experience as the Career Tracks system.  Although the HRMS will replace UCR developed HR tools, current functionality will be maintained, and where possible improved. 

Recognizing the importance and value of our end users’ unique perspectives, opportunities to incorporate user input into the design and implementation was made available.  Organization representatives can be found on the HRMS End Users – Organization Representatives webpage.

 ePerformance will replace UCR’s paper performance appraisal process and is designed to streamline the appraisal process from goal planning to performance assessments.  The implementation date is pending and will be announced in 2021.
TBD

UCR is continuing to evaluate the implementation of a new applicant tracking system, Talent Acquisition Manager (TAM).  TAM would replace UCR's current applicant tracking system, iRecruit. TAM allows for full-cycle recruitment and selection to include creation of job postings, managing and screening applicants, approving and delivering job offers.  Please check back for further updates.

Professional Development

In 2016 Human Resources launched a framework for staff professional development. We have added courses and programs to develop staff to be successful in their jobs and accomplish their career goals. Examples of new courses include:

  • Facilitator Skills Training
  • Presentation Skills
  • Business Writing
  • Collaborative Leadership
  • People Management Certificate Program (online)

Please take a moment to check out our course catalog.

In recognition of the campus commitment to ongoing professional development, the creation of an annual individual development plan for every employee is now part of the annual performance management process.

  • Market UCR as an employer of choice to graduating UCR students.
