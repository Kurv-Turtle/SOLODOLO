Other Opportunities to Support Students

Beyond the classroom

Reasons to support other opportunities for students

There are numerous ways to support USC students that go beyond scholarships to offset tuition and room and board expenses. When our students find themselves facing unexpected financial challenges, the university steps in to provide emergency financial support to ensure student safety security, and continued academic progress. In addition, when students seek to enrich their learning experiences through extracurricular activities, the university steps in to provide resources to help turn their ambitions into reality. Gifts from generous donors like you make these things possible.

Student Basic Needs Fund

Today more than ever before, many students need funds to help them overcome a food, housing, or financial emergency. Your support can help provide the financial security they need

Experiential Opportunities

Every year, students need funds to study abroad, attend conferences, participate in internships, conduct class projects and field research, as well as other endeavors. Many of these activities are not possible without support from generous donors.

“I’ve been really lucky over the past four years at USC. I’ve been able to travel to 14 countries across four continents. One of the biggest things I’ve learned at USC is to take every opportunity as it comes. I’m here for the journey and excited to see where I go.”

Ruby Au
USC Marshall School of Business

Help students reach their potential

USC’s most important mission is preparing students to excel. That mission begins with the partnership of donors like you who privately fund scholarships that help bring extraordinary students to our community—transforming their lives in the process.

“Thank you for your interest in supporting our students. I’d be happy to tell you more about the options available. Feel free to email me.”

Bill Holland
Senior Director of Special Programs
